Alan Turing: The Father of Modern Computing

Alan Turing was an English mathematician, computer scientist, logician, cryptanalyst, philosopher, and theoretical biologist who made many revolutionary contributions to modern science and technology. In 1936, he developed the idea for the Universal Turing Machine, the basis for the first computer, that later formulized the concepts of algorithms and computations. He made insightful contributions to the idea of artificial intelligence through his creation of the Turing-test which determined if a machine was intelligent by asking it a series of questions. Alan Turing is my inspiration and a rolemodel as he astronomically impacted mine and the lives of countless others.

Despite his acedemic prosperity and success, Turing faced numerous hardships. He was a gay man in a time of unfair laws against homosexuality. In 1952, he was arrested for “gross indecency” and subjected to the treatment of hormone injections. Nevertheless he continued with his studies and didn't let that distract him from his work. I consider him a hero becuase heros must use their strengths to improve the world while staying true to their values in the face of adversity.
